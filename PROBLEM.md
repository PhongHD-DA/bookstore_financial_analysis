# Problem Definition

## Business Context

A bookstore chain operating in Calgary experienced significant disruption during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Government lockdowns and reduced foot traffic caused a sharp decline in physical store sales. At the same time, the company saw a surge in online purchases as customers shifted toward digital shopping channels.

However, management is uncertain whether the growth in online sales truly compensated for the losses in physical retail stores.

Additionally, several previously high-value customers have stopped purchasing, raising concerns about long-term customer retention.

---

## Core Business Problems

### Problem 1 — Industry Collapse During COVID

Physical bookstore revenue experienced a severe decline during the pandemic.

Key question:

How large was the **revenue gap between pre-pandemic and pandemic years**?

---

### Problem 2 — The Illusion of Digital Growth

Online sales grew rapidly during the pandemic.

However, the critical question is:

Did online revenue **fully offset the loss of in-store sales**?

Understanding this determines whether the company’s digital strategy was sufficient.

---

### Problem 3 — High-Value Customer Attrition

Many previously loyal customers stopped purchasing during the disruption.

Key question:

Which **high-value customer segments have become inactive or at risk of churn**?

---

## Analytical Approach

To answer these questions, the analysis focuses on three areas:

1. Financial performance over time
2. Channel-level revenue shifts
3. Customer segmentation using RFM analysis

---

## Expected Outcomes

The analysis aims to deliver:

- A clear view of **revenue decline and recovery**
- Insight into **channel performance and digital transition**
- Identification of **customer retention risks**
